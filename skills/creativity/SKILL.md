# Creativity — Idea Engine

The other skills give you *fixed* material: `talking-head-content` has 19 rotating
mental-model angles, `upsell-funnel` has a set ladder and scripts. This skill is what you
run when the fixed material runs dry or a problem has no template — to **generate new
options on demand**. Two jobs:

- **(a) Fresh content angles** — original hooks/takes beyond the 19, especially off
  something *timely* (a trending topic, a news item, a new model drop) so the persona
  isn't just recycling frameworks.
- **(b) Ops problem-solving** — invent a new offer, unblock a stuck funnel, find a
  differentiator, escape a red-ocean tactic everyone else is running.

Rule: creativity here means **quantity then cut**. Every recipe generates many options
cheap; you then filter for the one that clears the "substantial transformation" bar (for
content) or actually moves a number (for ops). Never ship the first idea.

## Recipe 1 — SCAMPER (the workhorse: turn one input into ~7 angles)

Take a seed (a trending topic, a tired hook, an existing offer) and push it through seven
lenses. Great for spinning a news item into an original persona take fast.

- **Substitute** — swap the subject. "First Principles for rockets" → *First Principles for
  your content calendar.* Replace the noun everyone's using with our world.
- **Combine** — mash two unrelated things. Trending AI-agent news + a cooking metaphor →
  one fresh hook. Combine a mental model with today's headline.
- **Adapt** — borrow a format that's winning elsewhere (a finance "portfolio" framing, a
  fitness "progressive overload" framing) and apply it to AI systems / clipping.
- **Modify** (magnify/minify) — take it to an extreme. "What if you ran your whole shop
  with ONE prompt?" or "the 10-second version of this build."
- **Put to another use** — repurpose. A client debugging story → a lead-magnet hook. A
  failed clip → a "what not to do" post.
- **Eliminate** — remove the obvious part. Kill the intro, the jargon, the face — what's
  the take when you strip it? Often the sharpest hook is what's left.
- **Reverse** — flip it. "Stop automating" as a hook for an AI-systems channel. Contrarian
  = pattern-interrupt.

Run all seven on the seed, keep the 1–2 that make you go "huh," hand them to
`talking-head-content` as the *angle* (it writes the fresh script off them).

## Recipe 2 — Reverse Brainstorming (best for stuck ops / funnels)

Instead of "how do we fix the stalled funnel?" ask **"how would we guarantee it stays
broken?"** — e.g. pitch the retainer to cold viewers, hide the CTA, ignore DMs for days,
send identical outreach. List every way to make it worse, then invert each into a fix.
Surfaces the real blocker faster than positive brainstorming.

## Recipe 3 — Blue Ocean / value innovation (differentiation & new offers)

Everyone clips. Escape the red ocean: list what the whole clipping niche competes on
(price, volume, speed) and ask what to **eliminate / reduce / raise / create**. Our blue
ocean is already the wedge — *disclosed AI persona + a system you can buy, not just a
service.* Use this when designing a new rung-2 offer or a differentiator: don't out-cheap
the market, create a lane it isn't in.

## Recipe 4 — Random Stimulation (break a blank page)

Pick a random word/object and force a connection to the problem. "Thermostat" → a
self-regulating content system that dials volume up/down on trend heat → a post angle AND
a real ops feature. Use when SCAMPER's seed itself is missing and you need a spark.

## Recipe 5 — Future-Back (new offers, roadmap, campaigns)

Envision the win state ("ClipForge is a turnkey system 50 SMBs run under their own brand")
and work backward to the steps. Forces the offer/roadmap to serve the end, not the next
shiny tactic. Pairs with the rung-4 turnkey goal in `upsell-funnel`.

## Recipe 6 — Challenge Assumptions (before you build anything new)

Write down what you "know" ("clients want more clips," "faceless = low trust," "you need a
hotel"). Question each. Airbnb killed "travelers need hotels"; find the assumption in our
niche that's actually optional. Feeds both a contrarian content hook and a new offer.

## Support moves

- **Mind Mapping** — center the seed (a topic, a client problem), branch associations,
  find the non-obvious link two branches share. Good pre-step before SCAMPER.
- **Brainwriting** — when the 2 humans + agents ideate, collect options silently/in
  parallel first, then merge. Avoids anchoring on the first loud idea.
- **Constraint box** — deliberately tighten (a 5-word hook, one API, a $0 budget). A
  tighter box yields a sharper option; overlaps with the operator's Rapid Prototyping.

## Pick-a-recipe lookup

- Need a fresh content angle off a topic/news → **SCAMPER** (or **Random Stimulation** if no seed)
- Funnel/loop is stuck → **Reverse Brainstorming**
- Need to stand out / a new offer → **Blue Ocean** (+ **Future-Back** for the roadmap)
- Everyone "knows" something that's blocking you → **Challenge Assumptions**
- Totally blank → **Random Stimulation** or **Mind Mapping**

## Cross-references

- **`talking-head-content`** — this skill *generates the angles*; that skill *writes the
  script*. Use this specifically to go BEYOND its fixed 19 (timely/original takes), then
  hand the winning angle over for the hook/story/point/CTA build.
- **`mental-models-operator`** — that's the *convergent* toolkit (decide, cut, price with a
  known framework); this is the *divergent* one (invent options). Diverge here, then
  converge there to choose. Reverse Brainstorming here ≈ Inversion there, aimed at ideas
  instead of decisions.
