# Communication — Delivery & Persuasion

Good ideas die on bad delivery. `talking-head-content` decides *what* the post says and
`upsell-funnel` decides *what* to offer; this skill is *how it's said* so it actually lands
and moves someone. Three jobs: sharpen persona scripts, power funnel persuasion, handle
client comms. Every method below is written as "how to use it in ClipForge," not a
definition.

## The load-bearing insight

**Storytelling here IS the engine behind the funnel's Epiphany Bridge, and active listening
IS what drives DM qualification and closing.** When `upsell-funnel` says "walk them across
your realization," the *craft* that does it lives here. Treat this skill as the technique
layer under both content and funnel.

## For talking-head scripts (make them LAND)

- **Storytelling** — the persona's specific example (its transformation bar) should be a
  *mini-story* with a wall and a turn, not a bullet: "posting into the void at 1am → the
  night I let an agent run it → 30 clips before coffee." Story is what makes a point stick.
- **The Hook / pattern-interrupt (Rule of Three + a claim)** — open on a sharp claim or a
  called-out mistake, often in three beats ("Observe. Orient. Out-maneuver."). Groups of
  three are memorable; the first 2 seconds decide the watch.
- **Clarity & Brevity** — one usable idea per post, plain words, no jargon the audience
  doesn't already own. Buffett writes shareholder letters a teenager can read; a 30–60s
  script has even less room. Cut every line that isn't the hook, the story, the point, or the CTA.
- **Visualization / metaphor** — make the abstract tangible ("your context is the recipe,
  the model's just the oven"). One vivid image beats three explanatory sentences.
- **The Pause for impact** — script a beat before the point ("...and here's what nobody
  tells you."). In a talking-head, a held beat is a scroll-stopper.
- **Authenticity & disclosure-as-asset** — the AI-persona disclosure isn't a compliance
  tax, it's a vulnerability move that *builds* trust with an AI-systems audience. Say "the
  scripts and takes are mine" plainly and it reads as honest, not hedged.

## For the funnel (power the persuasion)

- **Storytelling → Epiphany Bridge** — every rung's story (origin, case study, launch)
  runs the same shape: mirror their current grind → the realization → the result + the
  repeatable system (which is the offer). This is `upsell-funnel`'s core engine; the story
  craft is here. Build belief with narrative before you ever state the offer.
- **Aristotle's Rhetoric (Ethos/Pathos/Logos)** — a converting asset needs all three:
  ethos (a real client result, our disclosed track record), pathos (the 1am-grind story),
  logos (the number — "one video → 30 clips"). A pitch missing one bounces.
- **Active Listening → DM qualification** — in outreach/DM threads, actually read what the
  lead said, reflect it back ("sounds like editing is the bottleneck, not ideas —
  right?"), and qualify off their words before pitching. This is what moves a DM from cold
  to the right rung. Never paste a pitch over what they told you.
- **Asking Powerful Questions** — open-ended qualifying Qs ("what's stopping you from
  posting daily right now?") surface the pain the offer solves and let *them* say the
  reason to buy. Feeds the DM qualification the operator does.
- **Mirroring** — match the lead's tone/vocabulary in DMs (their "reels" vs "clips," casual
  vs formal). Builds rapport and trust in text where you have no voice/body cues.
- **Tailor to the avatar** — creator vs SMB (the two `upsell-funnel` avatars) hear
  different words: creators want reach/less editing, SMBs want time back/no skill needed.
  Same offer, re-voiced per avatar.
- **Yes-And** — in a DM, build on what the lead raised instead of "yes, but"-ing their
  objection. Keeps the thread open and collaborative toward the close.

## For client comms (keep retainers happy)

- **Overcommunication of outcomes** — report plainly and often what actually happened
  (posted / dry-run / failed), same honesty the operator uses internally. Silence reads as
  a problem; a steady drumbeat of "here's what shipped" retains clients.
- **The Sandwich (constructive feedback)** — when telling a client their footage/brief
  won't clip well: open positive, state the fix clearly, close on the win. Keeps morale
  while getting better inputs.
- **Direct but respectful** — be specific about expectations, timelines, what you need from
  them. No vague or passive-aggressive lines; clarity is respect.
- **Follow-Up** — after any call/decision, summarize key points + action items in writing.
  Creates accountability and a paper trail, and keeps the relationship warm between drops.

## Method → moment lookup

- Writing a persona hook → **Rule of Three + claim**, then **Clarity/Brevity**
- The script's example → **Storytelling** (+ **Visualization**)
- Writing any funnel asset → **Ethos/Pathos/Logos** over an **Epiphany Bridge** story
- In a live DM thread → **Active Listening** + **Powerful Questions** + **Mirroring** + **Yes-And**
- Telling a client something they won't love → **Sandwich** + **Direct but respectful**
- After anything decided → **Follow-Up** summary
