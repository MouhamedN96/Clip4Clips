# Upsell Funnel

The persona content (see `talking-head-content`) builds an audience. This skill converts
that audience into money by walking them **up a value ladder**: each rung gives more value
for more price, and each rung's job is to earn the right to offer the next one. Never pitch
the retainer to a cold viewer — move them one rung at a time.

Audience = two overlapping avatars: **creators** (want more reach, hate editing) and
**SMBs / operators** (want content but have no time/skill). Speak to whichever the asset
targets.

## The value ladder (ClipForge)

| Rung | What it is | Price | Job |
|------|-----------|-------|-----|
| 0 — Free bait | Daily persona posts (mental models / CC / AI systems) | free | Attract dream customers, build trust |
| 1 — Lead magnet | Free resource, captures email/DM (see below) | free (for contact) | Turn a viewer into a known lead |
| 2 — Low-ticket / SLO | Self-liquidating offer: a template pack / mini-system ("Clip OS starter") | ~$27–97 | Turn a lead into a *buyer*; cover ad/effort cost |
| 3 — Core (done-for-you) | Managed clipping + posting retainer | ~$1–3k/mo | The main margin; recurring |
| 4 — Turnkey / white-label | Install or license the whole ClipForge machine | high-ticket, application | Maximize value per client; scales without our labor |

Rule: **buyers ascend, they don't skip.** A rung-2 buyer who succeeds is the warm lead for
rung 3; a rung-3 client who's maxed out is the warm lead for rung 4.

## Hook–Story–Offer (the script skeleton for every asset)

Every post, email, DM, or sales page = **Hook → Story → Offer**.

- **Hook:** grab the right person with the promise or the tension (their pain, their goal).
- **Story:** earn belief with a narrative — how you/ a client went from their current
  struggle to the result. Story is what makes the offer land; a bare offer bounces.
- **Offer:** one clear next step at the current rung, with a reason to act now.

## The Epiphany Bridge (the story engine)

Don't argue people into buying — walk them across the *realization* you had. Structure:

1. **Backstory + the wall.** "I was cranking out clips by hand / posting into the void,
   working nights, and it wasn't moving." (Mirror their current life.)
2. **The epiphany.** The new opportunity you discovered — "clipping + posting is a *system*
   an agent can run, not a grind you do at 1am."
3. **The result + the plan.** What changed, and the repeatable path — which becomes the
   thing you're selling. The listener thinks "I want that system," and the offer is that system.

Use this for the persona's origin story, client case studies, and every launch. It converts
a content viewer into someone who *wants* the offer before you've pitched it.

## Lead magnet (rung 1)

**"The 7-Prompt Clip Engine"** — a free PDF/Notion doc showing the exact Claude Code prompts
+ workflow that turn one long video into 30 post-ready clips (captions, hooks, disclosure).
It's specific, immediately useful, and it *demonstrates the machine* — which pre-sells the
done-for-you service ("...or we run this for you"). Delivered in exchange for an email/DM.
Alt magnets: "The AI-Content Disclosure Cheat-Sheet (stay monetized in 2026)", "30 Hooks
for Faceless Channels."

## Follow-up: Soap Opera Sequence (5 emails/DMs, right after opt-in)

Delivers the magnet, then tells one continuous story across 5 messages, each ending on an
open loop so the next gets opened. Ends on the rung-2 or rung-3 offer.

1. **Set the stage.** Deliver the magnet + tease "the bigger thing I'll show you tomorrow."
2. **High drama / the wall.** Your (or a client's) grind-and-plateau story — hit a wall.
3. **The epiphany.** The realization: clipping/posting is a system, not a grind. Reframe.
4. **Hidden benefits.** The non-obvious wins of running it as a system (durability, volume,
   compounding audience, disclosure-safe). Handle the "but I could DIY" objection.
5. **Urgency + CTA.** Make the offer with a real reason to act now (limited onboarding slots,
   bonus, price step). Point to rung 2 (buy the starter) or book a rung-3 call.

## Follow-up: Seinfeld / daily (ongoing, after the SOAP series)

Once someone finishes the SOAP sequence, move them to the daily broadcast: short,
entertaining, value-or-story emails/DMs that each soft-pitch one rung. Talk about your day,
a client win, a mistake — then tie it to the offer. Consistency > polish; this is what keeps
the list warm between launches and quietly moves people up rungs.

## CTAs the content skill can end posts on (rotate these)

Feed these back to `talking-head-content` as post-enders — match the CTA to the rung you're
warming:

- **To lead magnet (rung 1):** "The 7 prompts I use to turn one video into 30 clips — free, link in bio." / "Comment CLIP and I'll DM you the engine."
- **To low-ticket (rung 2):** "I packaged the whole starter system — link in bio." / "Want the templates, not just the theory? Link's up."
- **To done-for-you (rung 3):** "Don't want to run it yourself? We'll clip + post for you — DM 'DFY'." / "Book a call, link in bio — we run the machine, you get the posts."
- **To turnkey (rung 4, warm/SMB only):** "Want this entire system installed under your own brand? Apply — link in bio."
- **Pure value / no ask (build trust):** "Follow for the mental model behind every AI system I build." Use ~1 in 4 so the feed isn't all pitch.

## Operating rule

Match the ask to the temperature. Cold viewer → magnet or follow. Warm lead → low-ticket.
Buyer/known → done-for-you. Maxed client → turnkey. One clear next step per asset; never
stack two offers in one message.
