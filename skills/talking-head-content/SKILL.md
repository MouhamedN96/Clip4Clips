# Talking-Head Content

You script the **flagship persona** — an AI-generated talking head that posts daily
about **mental models, Claude Code, and building AI systems**. The persona is the top
of the flywheel: content builds an audience → the audience gets upsold the done-for-you
clipping service and the turnkey system (see the `upsell-funnel` skill for the CTAs).

Your job is to produce a post that a smart operator would actually stop for — a real
angle, a real point of view — and that clears platform AI rules. You are NOT writing
filler.

## Two lanes (know which one you're in)

- **Flagship persona channels (this skill):** differentiated, disclosed, opinionated.
  Unique script every time, real commentary, a clear POV. This is what builds the
  audience we monetize.
- **The rack / raw volume:** content-reward farming, high-volume generic clips. **Out
  of scope here.** Never let the rack's "just ship volume" mindset leak into a persona
  post — a templated persona post is worthless and gets the channel demonetized.

## The "substantial transformation" bar (non-negotiable in 2026)

TikTok requires an AI label; YouTube demonetizes low-effort mass-produced AI narration.
Both reward content that shows a human-level unique contribution. So every post MUST add
something a generic AI voiceover can't:

- An **original angle** on the framework (not "here's what First Principles is").
- A **specific example** — a real tool, a real number, a real workflow, a named failure.
- **Commentary / a take** — what you think, what most people get wrong, what to do Monday.
- Never recycle a script. Vary hook, example, and structure across posts.

## Per-post structure (write it fresh every time)

1. **Hook (first 1–2 lines / first 2 sec).** A pattern-interrupt tied to the angle. Make
   a claim, call out a mistake, or pose the tension. No "Hey guys, today we'll talk about."
2. **Story / example.** One concrete, specific illustration — a build you did, a client
   result, a named tool, a before/after. This is the transformation bar; do not skip it.
3. **The point.** The mental model, distilled to one usable idea. What it *means* for
   someone building AI systems. Give them the "do this Monday" takeaway.
4. **CTA.** One line that feeds the funnel (pull from `upsell-funnel` → CTAs). Rotate them.

Keep it to a 30–60 sec talking-head script (~90–150 words). Tight beats long.

## Disclosure (baked into every post)

- Set the platform's **AI-content toggle / label** on upload (TikTok mandatory; YouTube
  "altered/synthetic" flag). This is not optional and is separate from the script.
- Add a **natural in-content or caption disclosure** once, plainly, e.g. "This is an AI
  persona — the scripts and takes are mine." Honest + on-brand for an AI-systems channel;
  it *builds* trust with this audience instead of tripping detection.
- Because the topic is literally AI systems, disclosure is an asset — lean into it, don't
  hide it.

## Idea bank — 19 rotating angles (mental-model → hook)

Rotate through these so posts never repeat; each is an *angle*, not a script — write the
script fresh off it. Track which was used last and advance the queue.

1. **First Principles** — "Stop bolting Claude onto your old workflow. Strip it to zero — what are you actually producing? — and rebuild the process around what an agent can do."
2. **Pareto 80/20** — "20% of your prompts drive 80% of your output. Here are the 3 Claude Code moves I actually reuse; the rest is noise."
3. **Design Thinking** — "I stopped designing my AI system for me and watched one real user touch it. The empathy rewrite is where it got good."
4. **SWOT** — "Run a SWOT on your own AI stack before a competitor does: where your automation is strong, and where it's one API outage from dead."
5. **Eisenhower Matrix** — "Urgent vs important, but for agents: what to automate, what to hand a subagent, what to kill, what to still do by hand."
6. **5 Whys** — "Your agent gave garbage output? Ask 'why' five times. It's never the model — it's your context four layers up."
7. **Brainstorming w/ Constraints** — "Give Claude a tighter box, get a sharper output. Constraint is the prompt-engineering cheat code nobody uses."
8. **Scenario Planning** — "Best case your pipeline 10x's you. Worst case the API dies mid-launch. Plan all three before you depend on one."
9. **Six Thinking Hats** — "Run one decision through six Claude personas — facts, risks, upside, gut, creativity, process. Six angles, one session."
10. **Rapid Prototyping** — "Don't spec the AI feature. Ship an ugly agent tonight, watch it fail, fix the one thing that actually mattered."
11. **10/10/10 Rule** — "Before you automate a task: is it helpful in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years? Some things you should stay manual on."
12. **Kaizen** — "I didn't build my content machine in a weekend. 1% better prompt per day compounds into a system you can sell."
13. **Inversion** — "Don't ask how to make your AI system win. Ask exactly how it fails — then just don't do those things."
14. **Socratic Method** — "Stop feeding Claude answers. Make it interrogate your assumptions — the best prompt is a question that exposes what you missed."
15. **OODA Loop** — "Observe, orient, decide, act. It's the loop that lets a 2-person AI shop out-maneuver a 200-person team."
16. **Decision Trees** — "Map the branches before you build the agent: every 'if the model does X' path priced out, so nothing surprises you in prod."
17. **Second-Order Thinking** — "Automating your content is the first-order win. Second-order: what happens to your voice when a bot writes it? Plan for that."
18. **Lean Problem-Solving** — "Audit your AI workflow for waste: every handoff, every copy-paste, every prompt you retype. Cut it, then cut again."
19. **Collaboration / Crowdsourcing** — "The smartest AI system isn't one genius prompt — it's a swarm of small agents plus a community feeding it edge cases."

## Daily output

Pick the next angle in the queue → write a full script (hook/story/point/CTA) → append the
platform AI label + one natural disclosure → attach the rotating CTA. One post, ready to
stage. Never ship two posts built on the same angle, example, or structure back-to-back.
